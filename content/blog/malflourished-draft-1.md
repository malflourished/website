Well it took sixteen years for me to put work up online, but we're finally here. It only took until AI was ready before I could accomplish this. There's something very exciting about creating a space where I can put my thoughts, creative work and experiences down in a digital space I can call my own. There's also an equally deep and unsettling feeling that has come about over the last 5 years. As LLM's have matured, and their capabilities  have become more and more obvious, as well as the implications here. If it can be brought to life through pixels, it can be created by a machine. 

I go back and forth about the implications here, and this fundamental shift in how everything will be created going forward. As a tool, it can be an amazing thought-partner, an assistant, a file organizer, etc. Or it can just as easily replace your thoughts, your decisions or your creativity. Every month a better model comes out -- one that can generate videos, images, storyboards, documents, scripts, recipes, lists -- anything that can be translated through pixels, it can do it better than me. 

I've created a digital space that I'm proud of, and now everyone can as well. I didn't build it -- I instructed a machine to. And now that the machine has built it I have no true sense of accomplishment over this site. It laid dormant in my mind for 16 years -- my creative experiences and ideas slowly building over the years, with no home online. It took artificial intelligence to pull it out of my mind and finally put it into structure, a form online made of pixels. I didn't make this site, I instructed it. The work is my own, the thoughts are my own, the creations are my own...right? I think so. What's more important -- the idea or the product? What's actually out there in the world, who can build, who can make themselves known. 

I'm not sure if I'm an optimist or a pessimest here. I have a site that would not hvae ever made, images that I would have never created, ideas fleshed out that I would have never spent the time to entertain. Are these creations as important to me, knowing how much aide I've gotten? 

This is what being malflourished means. It's never been easier create, it's also never been harder to feel a sense of accomplishment. Just as the barriers to creating are crumbling down, the feeling of accomplishment, hardship, breakthru's -- to overcome, has never felt harder. 

The proliferation of creative flourishing is about to begin, the human role is just no longer the main one.
